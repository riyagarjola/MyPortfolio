About

I don't just build software — I can explain, defend, and publish why it works.

I'm a Computer Science undergraduate at Graphic Era Hill University, currently maintaining an 8.9/10 CGPA. My work sits at the intersection of solid software engineering fundamentals — data structures, operating systems, databases — and applied AI, where I've published research on generative adversarial network training and NLP-based text classification.

Outside coursework, I mentor learners in cloud architecture as a Google Cloud Arcade Mentor, and was selected as a SheFi Scholar in 2025 for excellence in technology and leadership. I care about building things that measurably work: faster search, higher model accuracy, lower latency — not just demos.
